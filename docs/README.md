# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

## Summary

This README offers a concise introduction to OctoAcme's project management processes and provides links to all major process documents. It helps new and current team members understand workflow, decision rationale, and artifacts for each phase of a typical project.

---

## Project Management Process Overview

OctoAcme uses a structured set of process documents to guide teams from initial idea to continuous improvement. All cross-functional projects that deliver product features, services, or integrations follow this lifecycle.

### Principles

- **Customer-first**: prioritize customer value and usability.
- **Iterative delivery**: deliver small, testable increments.
- **Clear ownership**: each project has a named Project Manager (PM) and Product Lead.
- **Data-informed decisions**: measure impact and iterate based on evidence.
- **Psychological safety**: encourage feedback and learning.

### Core Roles

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Project Manager (PM)** | Coordinates delivery, schedules, risk, and communications. |
| **Product Manager (PdM)** | Defines outcomes, prioritizes backlog, and measures success. |
| **Developers** | Implement features, collaborate on design and testability. |
| **QA/Testing** | Validate quality and acceptance criteria. |
| **Stakeholders** | Provide inputs and approvals. |

### Project Lifecycle

1. **Initiation** — Problem statement, stakeholder alignment, high-level timeline.
2. **Planning** — Scope, resources, milestones, dependencies.
3. **Execution** — Build, test, review, and iterate.
4. **Release** — Deploy, verify, and announce.
5. **Close & Retrospective** — Capture learnings and next steps.

## Phase Summaries

### Initiation
Define and validate the project idea, align stakeholders, and produce a lightweight project one-pager. The goal is to confirm business need, identify success criteria, and reach a go/no-go decision before planning begins.

### Planning
Turn an approved initiative into an actionable backlog and release plan. Activities include a kickoff meeting, backlog prioritization and estimation, Definition of Done, and a dependency and risk map.

### Execution & Tracking
Manage day-to-day delivery through daily standups, a structured PR workflow, CI-backed quality gates, and regular demos. Track velocity, escalate blockers through defined levels, and keep the risk register current.

### Risk Management & Communication
Maintain a risk register throughout the project. Assess and mitigate risks, provide regular stakeholder updates, and follow defined escalation paths (Team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor).

### Release & Deployment
Standardize how features reach production. Patch, minor, and major releases each follow pre-release checklists, automated pipelines, smoke tests, and a rollback/incident playbook.

### Retrospective & Continuous Improvement
After each sprint, release, or major milestone, hold a timebox retrospective to surface what went well, what can improve, and 2–3 prioritized action items. Track improvements in the backlog and measure their impact.

### Roles & Personas
Defines the detailed responsibilities, goals, and typical communication patterns for Developers, Product Managers, and Project Managers. Used to frame scenarios in Copilot Spaces exercises.
